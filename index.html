Why some sellers choose a direct sale instead of listing

Listing can be the better move for some properties. A direct sale usually wins when convenience matters more than squeezing out the last dollar.

When listing may make more sense

You have time, the property shows well, repairs are manageable, and your main goal is maximizing sale price.

When a direct sale may make more sense

The property needs work, the situation is time-sensitive, or you want a simpler sale without open houses, contractor bids, and buyer fallout.

Common questions

Straight answers to the things sellers usually want to know first.

Do I need to repair or clean up the property first?

No. Harris Homes reviews properties as-is, including ones with deferred maintenance, problem tenants, inherited contents, or cosmetic issues.

How fast can this move?

That depends on the property and your timeline. Some situations move quickly, while others need a little more review. The point is flexibility, not pressure.

Will I be pushed into an offer?

No. If the fit is not there, you can walk away. The goal is a clear conversation and an option that makes sense for your situation.

What kinds of properties do you look at?
